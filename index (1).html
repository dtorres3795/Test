About This Tool

This website creates strong, completely random passwords to help keep your accounts secure. Each password is 16 characters long and uses a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters.

Why Strong Passwords Matter

Weak passwords are one of the most common reasons for account breaches. A strong password should be long and contain unique characters. Avoid reusing passwords across multiple accounts and platforms.

How It Works

The generator selects from a list of different characters and uses JavaScript's Math.random() to create a unique, completely randomized password. Every click produces 6 completely new passwords instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should a password be?

At least 16 characters. The longer the better — length is one of the most important factors in password strength.

Should I reuse passwords?

Never. If one account is breached, reusing passwords means all your other accounts become vulnerable too.

How often should I change my password?

You should only change a password if you suspect it has been compromised or if a service you use has been breached.

What makes a password weak?

Short length, common words, predictable patterns like "123456" or "password", and reusing passwords across multiple sites all make a password weak.
